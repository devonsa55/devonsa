members in case of emergency, to coordinate family logistics, and receive regular location updates via SMS (all without the need to install an app). Our key demographic are parents with kids 8-17. After months of research, we categorized these parents into different personas \u2013&nbsp;\"Schedulers\", \"Controllers\" &amp; \"Just-in-case\" users.&nbsp;</h3><h3>While Family Locator saw initial success at it's launch in 2009, emerging technologies and shifting consumer expectations meant that we needed to redesign our platform to compete in 2017 for Sprint, AT&amp;T and T-Mobile.</h3><p>\u00A0</p><h2><strong>Goals</strong></h2><p><strong>Design</strong></p><ol><li><h3>Reframe existing features using new design principles born from user insights to <strong>improve features adoption and boost 1 month retention.&nbsp;</strong></h3></li><li><h3><strong>Create an extensible platform</strong> for future development to win new contracts with more carrier partners around the world.&nbsp;</h3></li><li><h3>Modernize the UI to optimize for new features that utilize new technologies.&nbsp;</h3></li></ol><p><strong>Business</strong></p><ul><li><h3>Win over our carrier partners with an enticing future vision for the product.</h3></li><li><h3>Maintain and grow the user base with new value in the product.&nbsp;</h3></li><li><h3>Improve retention &amp; LTV</h3></li></ul><h3>\u00A0</h3><h2><strong>Role </strong></h2><h3>Lead designer on a 25 person team performing \u2013&nbsp;needfinding, product roadmap prioritization with PMs,&nbsp;wireframing,&nbsp;UI, prototyping, usability, copywriting and some visual design.</h3><h3>\u00A0</h3><h2><strong>Summary</strong></h2><h3>In home interviews, diving into analytics, and collecting survey data suggested that we needed to update our assumptions the product team was making about our 3 personas we design for. By creating journies for each persona, we were able to understand the details of each of our persona's journeys that needed improvement &amp; build empathy for our users across the engineering and product teams. Through our efforts we brought more value to all parents by adding robust new features, contextualizing existing features to increase their usability &amp; adoption, and by creating a more extensible UI framework that simplified integration of new features in the product roadmap. All while balancing an aggressive timeline, getting carrier buy in and validating our most critical assumptions along the
